The Impatient List

What would it be like to be waiting for a kidney transplant in the United States?

More than 114,000 Americans eligible for organ transplants are waiting for a donation, with 80% of them waiting for a kidney transplant.

Kidneys filter and remove excess waste, minerals and fluid from the blood. When your kidneys lose this filtering ability due to, for example, diabetes, harmful levels of fluid and waste accumulate in your body. Your blood pressure will then raise and result in kidney failure. When the kidneys have lost about 90 percent of their ability, end-stage kidney disease occurs. When your kidneys fail, kidney transplantation is one of two ways to replace the work your own kidneys can no longer do. Compared to its alternative, dialysis, kidney transplant offers more freedom and better quality of life.

However, getting a transplantation is not easy.

To receive a transplant, you must first register yourself on the waitlist of a transplant center.
